- Contraction: star material begins to contract until hydrogen burning starts
	- radius decreases
	- temperature increases as the star contracts
0 yrs old

- Begin Main Sequence: star burns hydrogen at the core and remains stable while on main sequence
	- radius stays the same
	- temperature increases as hydrogen in burned
2.15e5 yrs model 280 profile 7

- TAMS: hydrogen has run out, star has left main sequence, preparation for red giant
	- star is not hot enough to burn helium yet
	- core begins to contract
	- hydrogen is burned in the shells of the star

- Core Helium Burning: star is red giant, helium burning
	- core is hot enough that helium burning begins
	- stars radius expands, star shells begins to cool and turn red
1.48e7 yrs model 379 profile 11

- Core Helium Exhaustion: helium burning runs out
	- star begins to slowly lose mass
1.647e7 yrs model 670

- Advanced Burning (CNO)
	- Helium has been transformed into Carbon and Oxygen
	- Core is hot enough to burn carbon
	- fuel is burned through very quick as they require high temperature to burn and 	release less energy compared to helium and hydrogen burning
1.65e7 yrs model 680

Core begins to deviate between radiation pressure, and carbon and oxygen burning

- Core Collapse:
	- core fuel is used up
	- Core is made up of Iron
	- gravity overcomes pressure and the core collapses in on itself
	- happens very quickly
	- star becomes a neutron star
1.65e7 yrs model 1000 profile 27


The star has a much shorter lifetime than smaller stars as it's core burns fuel much quicker. The core is able to get hot enough to burn and form iron, which leads to the ultimate collapse of the star and supernova.
